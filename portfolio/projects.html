Match 3 Connect 4

  • A small game idea of adding the token removal mechanic of match 3 games, into the typical connect 4 board game.
  • Made with HTML/CSS/JavaScript
  • The win condition is the same as connect 4; get at minimum 4 of the same color in a row, be it horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
  • The mix up is, every token has 1 of 3 different symbols on them. If a minimum of 3 tokens in a row, horizontally or vertically, have the same symbol, they are removed from the game as per match 3 rules.
  • Can be played here, against an AI, or turn by turn with a friend.
Game previews showcasing the combination of mechanics.

Scripting and Functionality

  • There are no images, anything that isn't text has been created with CSS.
  • Figuring out how to detect color matches in 8 directions, to determine if a win condition has been met.
  • Figuring out how to detect symbol matches in 4 directions, to determine if tokens should be removed.
  • A convincing enough enemy AI to play against in the event a 2nd player isn't available.

Trials and Tribulations

  • This was my first actual web project, made for the purpose of learning web development, in as full a capacity as I could reach alone.
  • There was an online multiplayer version, since removed because I have not found a new free host for my NodeJS server, after Heroku changed their terms of service, essentially eliminating all their free users.
  • Learning anything for the first time is challenging, although things felt especially obtuse with CSS, at least initially. Comparitively, I used my experience in Unity C# to springboard my education in JavaScript.
  • Learning to delay actions with Asyncs, Awaits, and timers, allowing players to make sense of the results of actions. Stepping away from a game engine's update loop was an interesting experience, getting me to understand time is what I make of it in a project of my own making.
  • The enemy AI was especially interesting, learning of a technique to simulate actions and score them against each other, in order to find the optimal action to take. For anything complex, I would expect this technique to require heavy abstraction of complex data, to run simulations at a reasonable rate.
