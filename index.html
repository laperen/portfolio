More about Me

I started my road to designing games when I was 13 years old, for fun. My first endeavor was a modification to Checkers/Draughts, which naturally failed horribly.

Undeterred, I continued modifying the rules of all sorts of common board and card games such as Chess, Snakes & Ladders, Monopoly, and UNO. These modifications although by no means worth noting did teach me, even the slightest change to the rules will affect the play experience.

After much modification and gaining enough confidence when I was 18, I decided to try making rules for a game of my own design. Positive feedback from my peers solidifed my decision to enrole into DigiPen to continue my game design journey into digital games.

After graduating from DigiPen, my work somehow or another has had me gravitate more towards coding rather than design. Not that it's a problem, just a specialization I did not expect to take considering the course I came from. I am however grateful for it, giving me more freedom to create almost any feature I envision.
